Keeping the Lights On: Europe's Energy Problem

Most European Countries are highly dependent on imports. The map above displays this in the form of Energy Dependence, which is calculated by taking net energy imports and dividing that by the gross energy consumption. In 2020, the only country in the EEA (European Economic Area) with a negative dependence was Norway. Energy Dependence over time can be explored by clicking on the map.

For this year, the top 5 Most Energy Dependent Countries in Europe are:

  1. Malta
  2. Cyprus
  3. Luxembourg
  4. Greece
  5. Georgia

The Decline of North Sea gas desposits has compelled European countries to import more of their natural gas from abroad, particularly from Russia. The diplomatic fallout from Russia's recent invasion of Ukraine has put this supply into question. You can explore natural gas imports by clicking on the highlighted countries.

Natural Gas is one of the most vital energy products, particularly for heating and electricity production. For example, in 2020 Italy imported 2.53 M Terrajoules of natural gas, primarily for household usage and electricity production.
